1. President Trump's inauguration drew fewer television viewers in the United States (30.6 million) than President Barack Obama did in 2009 (38 million) and President Ronald Reagan did in 1981 (42 million), Nielsen reported. 
  2. Many Europeans once scorned the potato; in France, merely growing the tuber, originally from South America, was illegal between 1748 and 1772.
  3. The term ''boycott'' was first popularized in the 1880s by an American journalist in Ireland. Within three months, it had spread as far as Salt Lake City.
  4. A modern commercial jetliner can have up to six million components that must be engineered and tested to safety standards before assembly starts.
  5. In the days of the Underground Railroad, at least 30,000 fugitive slaves fled the American South and made their way to Canada. The overwhelming majority settled in the southwestern portion of Ontario.
  6. The typical American family spends 29 percent of its after-tax income on child-care costs, compared with 10 percent or less in many other Western democracies.
  7. Immigrants to the United States are less likely to commit violent crimes than are people who were born here.
  8. Basketball shoes with herringbone-patterned soles are squeakier than ones with nubby soles.
  9. Corned beef takes its name from the salt that was originally used to brine it, the crystals so large they resembled kernels of corn.
  10. Coral reefs are found in shallow waters in only 0.2 percent of the oceans, but they support one-quarter of all marine life.
  11. In what is known as the ''halo effect,'' European communities are most likely to vote for far-right politicians if they live close to diverse areas, but not actually within them.
  12. By agreement, pandas provided by China to overseas zoos are considered loans, and their offspring must be sent to China before their fourth birthday in preparation for breeding.
  13. The shoe designer Manolo Blahnik grew up in the Canary Islands; as a child, he made tinfoil shoes for iguanas.
  14. Downloads of the app Signal, an open-source secure-messaging service, increased 400 percent in the days following the election.
  15. Female Supreme Court justices speak less often and use fewer words than male justices -- and yet are interrupted during oral argument at a significantly higher rate.
  16. Those on the Forbes 400 list of the richest Americans -- all billionaires, not counting the 100 or so billionaires who didn't make the cut -- have wealth greater than the bottom 61 percent of Americans.
  17. The media-measurement firm mediaQuant calculated that Donald J. Trump received the equivalent of $5.8 billion in free media over the course of the election, $2.9 billion more than Hillary Clinton.
  18. 97 percent of apparel sold in the United States is now made overseas, according to the American Apparel & Footwear Association.
  19. Before the Airline Deregulation Act of 1978, the government controlled airfares and routes (and airlines vied to differentiate themselves with meals and legroom).
  20. Nearly two billion people use Facebook every month, about 1.2 billion of them daily.
  21. Some 52,000 Americans die annually from drug overdoses, more than 10 times the number who died in the entire Iraq war.
  22. In 2000, only five countries counted China as their largest trading partner; today, more than 100 countries do, including the United States.
  23. The Pirahã, a small group of hunter-gatherers who live deep in the Amazon rain forest, speak a language that has no precise number words, not even ''one'' or ''two.''
  24. Hackers once breached an oil company through a PDF of a Chinese takeout menu.
  25. The number of people over 50 who cohabit with an unmarried partner jumped 75 percent from 2007 to 2016 -- the highest increase in any age group.
  26. According to a recent Pew Research Center report, about 89 percent of Democrats say the news media plays a role in holding political leaders to account, while about 42 percent of Republicans said the same. It is the widest gap ever recorded.
  27. The United States spends $3.1 billion a month in Afghanistan.
  28. Amazon accounted for 43 cents of every dollar spent online in the United States last year.
  29. As much as 50 percent of the greenhouse gas emissions created by the wool industry might come from sheep burps. (They contain methane, a powerful greenhouse gas.)
  30. The $20 bill is the most counterfeited bill in the United States.
  31. The median age in Maine is 43, the oldest in the nation.
  32. The first woman executed by the United States government was one of the conspirators in the assassination of President Lincoln. She was hanged in 1865.
  33. It's illegal to own just one guinea pig in Switzerland.
  34. Textual representations of laughter go back at least to Chaucer, who fancied the onomatopoeic ''haha'' to convey merriment in his writing. (Shakespeare preferred a more staccato ''ha, ha, he.'')
  35. Eighteen of the 20 tallest buildings in Hong Kong were built within the last 20 years.
  36. Florida has more lightning than any other state in the country (20.8 strikes per square mile) and the most people who die from lightning (54 since 2007 -- more than double that of the next state, Texas), according to the National Weather Service.
  37. More than half of the world's oceans belong to no one and are considered a ''pirate zone.''
  38. Google processes more than three billion search queries a day.
  39. At 118 decibels, Big Ben's bongs are over the human pain threshold and louder than a jet taking off.
  40. The cry of a baby kangaroo sounds like a cough.
  41. The United States director of the International Knife Throwers Hall of Fame is named Jack Dagger.
  42. After Pope Paul VI addressed the United Nations in 1965, he donated his diamond-encrusted papal ring and diamond and emerald cross to the U.N., so they could be sold to help the poor. At one point, the stunt driver Evel Knievel owned them.
  43. The New York City Department of Parks and Recreation oversaw 720 vasectomies of male deer on Staten Island last year.
  44. Louisa Adams, the London-born wife of President John Quincy Adams, spent much of her time in the White House in the 1820s depressed and binge-eating chocolates, according to historical accounts.
  45. There are more payday loan stores in the United States than there are McDonald's restaurants.
  46. Drug overdoses are the leading cause of death among Americans under 50.
  47. 165 different languages are spoken in Queens, New York.
  48. The blue light emitted by an iPad suppresses your brain's natural release of melatonin, the hormone that induces sleepiness, by over 50 percent.
  49. When Martha Stewart travels, she brings three iPads: one for books, one for TV series, and one for movies.
  50. Fewer than a third of hotel guests leave any money for the housekeepers.
  51. There are only four metropolitan areas in the United States where more than half of homes are priced above $500,000. All four are in California.
  52. For each person on earth, there are an estimated 17 million flies.
  53. For the first time since the 1960s, living with parents is the most common household arrangement for the 18-to-34-year-old age group.
  54. Google and Facebook controlled 70 percent of the digital advertising market in 2016.
  55. About 50 original movies made their debuts on Netflix in 2017.
  56. Cookie dough chunks extracted from ice cream pints don't bake up like the real thing because the ingredients in the dough are not only pasteurized but also heat-treated.
  57. Oscars are voted on by the Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences, an organization with a membership that is 72 percent male and 87 percent white.
  58. False smiles activate fewer facial muscles than sincere ones.
  59. The Warriors' Stephen Curry has a shoe calendar to tell him what sneakers to wear for which game.
  60. An emu, one of the biggest birds alive today, has a brain that weighs about as much as an AA battery.
