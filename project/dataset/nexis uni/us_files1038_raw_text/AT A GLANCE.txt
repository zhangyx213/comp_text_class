CITYWIDE - The ASPCA has launched its Operation Pit program, offering free spay, neuter or vasectomy surgeries on Thursdays to healthy pit bulls and pit bull mixes ages 3 months to 6 years at the ASPCA Bergh Memorial Animal Hospital, 424 E. 92nd St. "One of the most effective ways to manage pet overpopulation is to provide free spay/neuter," said Dr. Louise Murray, interim director of the Eastside ASPCA Hospital.
The program also includes free vaccinations and microchipping. Appointments are necessary. Call (877) 900-7487 or visit .
DOWNTOWN - Curious about space travel? Come to a free panel discussion "Solar Sailing: Pathway to the Stars," on Wednesday at 6 p.m. in City Tech's Atrium Amphitheater, 300 Jay St.
Hear speakers from The Planetary Society; Japan's JAXA Space Exploration Center; NASA and others. For more information, visit .
CONEY ISLAND - The Boilermaker Jazz Band will get swinging at Asser Levy Park, W. Fifth St. and Surf Ave., at 7:30 p.m. on Tuesday as part of Councilman Domenic Recchia Jr.'s summer concert series. For information, call (718) 373-9673 or visit .
BROOKLYN HEIGHTS - Learn how to stay on top of the latest technology breakthroughs at a small-business forum on Thursday at the Brooklyn Business Library, 280 Cadman Plaza West.
The forum is free, open to all businesses and will include a panel discussion and three seminars from 8:30 a.m. to 12:30 p.m. Call Ken Varga at (718) 875-1000, extension 133, or visit .
