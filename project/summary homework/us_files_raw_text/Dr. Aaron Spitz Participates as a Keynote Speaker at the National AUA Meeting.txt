Jun 08, 2015( ReleaseWire: / Delivered by Newstex) <nl/> Laguna Hills, CA -- (ReleaseWire) -- 06/08/2015 -- The American Urological Association (AUA) is the organization that binds and governs practicing urologists throughout the United States. Every year, the AUA holds annual meetings to ensure the proper regulation of urological practices, as well as to introduce new methodologies, treatments and drugs into the urological community. 
At their most recent meeting from May 15 to 19, 2015, Dr. Aaron Spitz, a leading urologist practicing in California, served as a keynote speaker, speaking at the opening of the conference. <nl/><nl/>The AUA carefully governs urology, paying full attention to the wide scope of the practice area. Urology involves the diagnosis and treatment for diseases and disorders of the male and female urinary tract, as well as male reproductive organs. To be an urologist requires an in-depth knowledge of internal medicine, gynecology and pediatrics, just to name a few. Urologists also specialize in the fields of Urologic Oncology (Cancer), Renal (Kidney) Transplantation, Male Infertility, Calculi (urinary tract/kidney stones) and Neurourology, or the relation of psychology to urological dysfunction. <nl/><nl/>Dr. Aaron Spitz is a board-certified urologist and the lead practitioner at the Orange County Urological Associates, as well as the head of their male infertility treatment division, "Vasectomy Reversal USA."[1] For years, Dr. Spitz has treated a variety of patients at the practice, becoming a widely trusted source for vasectomies and vasectomy reversals throughout San Diego and all over the nation. Dr. Spitz serves as the AUA's primary spokesperson, and as the AUA's lead delegate to the AMA House of Delegates, a force that serves as a governing body for medical professionals as a whole. <nl/><nl/>Dr. Spitz also serves as the President Elect of the California Urological Association, and he sits on the AUA Health Policy Council. He also works as an editor for the Journal of Endourology and the Journal of Andrology. Dr. Spitz also serves as an educator, working as an assistant clinical professor at U.C.
