EDITORS' PICK
How the Rock's life was changed by a Pa. football coach
Dwayne "The Rock" Johnson was not supposed to be using the bathroom in the teacher's lounge of his Pennsylvania high school. But he was a cocky kid. And an encounter with a miffed football coach who discovered Johnson where he had no business being wound up saving that troubled kid, now a massive star. Matt Breen tells the inspiring story.
CULINARY MILESTONE
Same yellow cart, but a neat new location
The award-winning taqueria South Philly Barbacoa has moved two doors up the street into Casa Mexico, a larger venue chef Cristina Martinez opened two years ago. Martinez told our Mike Klein the move is a consolidation, not a closing. Barbacoa's small menu is still served out of the familiar yellow cart, but it's now set up in Casa Mexico. A look at an openly undocumented immigrant marking 10 years as a restaurateur.
STEADYYYYYY!
A delicate surgery at an unstable time
Did you hear about the Montgomery County man who got a vasectomy during Friday's earthquake? No, seriously! Health reporter Aubrey Whelan caught up with him after the shaking stopped. Let's just say his sense of humor is intact.
PHL
A look at Philadelphia International Airport's budget wish list
Airport officials have presented a $556 million budget proposal to Philadelphia City Council. The spending plan is about $11 million more than fiscal 2024's. Business writer Ariana Perez-Castells gives us a look at what upgrades they have in mind to receive visitors in 2026, when Philadelphia will be host to FIFA World Cup soccer games, the baseball All-Star Game, and the 250th anniversary of the Declaration of Independence.
ENTERTAINMENT
Why Roots Picnic thrives and Made in america doesn't
For the second year in a row, one of Philadelphia's two major hip-hop-focused festivals has been called off. Roc Nation, Jay-Z's entertainment company, has pulled the plug on Made In America, the annual Labor Day weekend fest. Meanwhile, the Roots Picnic continues to be a summer mainstay. Music critic Dan DeLuca went looking for answers.
